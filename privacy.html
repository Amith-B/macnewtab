Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 2025

Overview

macNewTab is a Chrome extension that provides a macOS-style new tab experience with widgets for clock, calendar, search, and quick links. This extension is designed purely for personal convenience and does not collect or store any personal information.

Google Sign-In

macNewTab uses Google Sign-In only to let users access their Google Calendar events securely within the extension. The sign-in process is handled entirely by Google’s own authentication interface. The extension never has access to your password or sensitive credentials.

Permissions

The extension requests only the minimum permissions required to function properly:

  • identity — to allow optional Google Sign-In and identify the logged-in user for displaying their calendar events.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.readonly — to read upcoming events from your Google Calendar (if enabled).
  • storage — to locally store small preferences like layout mode, theme, or enabled widgets.
  • chrome_url_overrides — to replace the default new tab page with the macOS-style interface.

All data accessed using these permissions stays entirely on your device. macNewTab does not send or share any information with external servers.

Data Collection and Usage

macNewTab does not collect, log, or transmit any personal data. Any data retrieved via Google APIs (such as calendar events) is displayed only within your local browser session and not stored or shared externally.

Data Protection Mechanisms

Since macNewTab does not transmit Google user data (calendar events) to any external server, the primary protection mechanism is the data remaining on your device. We adhere to the following standards to ensure data protection:

  • Encryption in Transit: All communication between the extension and Google's APIs (e.g., fetching calendar events) is done exclusively over secure, encrypted channels using **HTTPS/TLS**.
  • Data Minimization: We only request **read-only** access to your calendar data, limiting the scope of access to the minimum required functionality.
  • Local Storage: Any temporary or cached calendar data is stored only in your local browser storage, protected by your browser's security measures.
  • No Credentials Stored: We utilize Google's secure OAuth 2.0 protocol and do not store or ever have access to your Google password or sensitive account credentials.

Data Retention and Deletion Policy

macNewTab is designed not to retain any sensitive Google user data on any external servers. Therefore, data retention and deletion are controlled entirely by the user on their device and via Google’s account controls.

  • Retention: Google user data (calendar events) is only retained temporarily in your browser's memory and local storage for the duration of your use of the extension. All local data is deleted automatically upon uninstallation of the extension.
  • Deletion (Revoking Access): You can immediately and permanently delete the extension's access to your Google Calendar data at any time by visiting your Google Account Security Settings and revoking access for macNewTab.
  • Deletion (Uninstalling): Uninstalling the macNewTab Chrome extension automatically and permanently deletes all locally stored preferences, settings, and cached Google user data from your browser.

Third-Party Services

macNewTab relies solely on official Google APIs and Chrome APIs. For more details on how Google handles your account data, please review Google’s Privacy Policy.

Contact

For any questions or concerns regarding this Privacy Policy, please contact the developer via the Chrome Web Store listing or through the project’s GitHub repository.
